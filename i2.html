The Fun Way to Learn How to Prevent Disasters

A game to educate about disaster prevention and mitigation

Rules of the Game

How to Play:

Begin the game by placing all tokens on the starting line. Taking turns, players advance by rolling the die and moving ahead by the number of spaces indicated.

The first player to reach the finish line is the winner. If the die roll is higher than the number of spaces to the finish line, the player must advance to the final space and then move back as many spaces as are left over.

Special Spaces:

  • Question Spaces (Blue): When landing on these spaces, answer a question correctly to roll again.
  • Surprise Spaces (Red): Follow the instructions provided when landing on these spaces.
  • Instruction Spaces (Green): Some spaces contain instructions written directly on them. Follow these instructions.

Purpose:

Riskland helps you understand how certain actions can reduce the impact of disasters, while others can increase our vulnerability. As you follow the path, you'll learn about disaster prevention and how to keep your community safe.

About Riskland

"Riskland" is an educational board game that deals with disaster prevention. The game conveys messages that help you understand how some actions can reduce the impact of disasters, while others can increase our vulnerability.

This digital version maintains the educational value of the original board game while making it accessible to more people through web browsers.

The game covers various hazards including:

  • Earthquakes
  • Floods
  • Tsunamis
  • Volcanic eruptions
  • Landslides
  • Hurricanes and tornadoes

By playing Riskland, you'll learn practical steps to prepare for and mitigate disasters in your community.
